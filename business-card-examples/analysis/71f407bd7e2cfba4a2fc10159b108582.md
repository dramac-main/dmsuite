#### Interpretation: The Interlocking "AV" Construction

The monogram is constructed from **two flat parallelogram ribbon-bands** that create an **impossible/Penrose-like interlocking**:

1. **Band A (left-leaning, forms the "A" shape):**
   - Starts from the TOP CENTER (y_card≈34%, x_card≈43-52%)
   - Descends left at ~60° from horizontal
   - Reaches leftmost extent at y_card≈58% (x_card≈35%)
   - The band is ~8-12% of card width (ribbon thickness)

2. **Band B (right-leaning, forms the "V" shape):**
   - Appears at y_card≈42% on the RIGHT (x_card≈60-65%)
   - Descends from the top right
   - Both bands share the SAME TOP APEX (the V/A point)
   - Reaches bottom at y_card≈67% (x_card≈48-54%)

3. **Crossing points (over/under illusion):**
   - **Crossing #1** at y_card≈42-46%: Right arm (V) passes BEHIND left arm (A)
     - Evidence: Left arm groups remain continuous while right arm appears fragmented
   - **Crossing #2** at y_card≈54-58%: Arms swap — left arm (A) passes BEHIND right arm (V)
     - Evidence: Left arm splits into leading edge + gap, while right arm is continuous
   - This alternating over/under creates the impossible-object / Celtic-knot effect

4. **Overall silhouette**: Diamond/rhombus oriented vertically — pointed at top (V apex) and pointed at bottom (A point), widest in the middle where both arms are visible

5. **Aspect ratio in card space**: Width 30.8% × Height 33.0% → slightly taller than wide, but because card is 1.75:1, the actual monogram shape is ~1.63:1 width:height (wider than tall)

## 4. THE INTERLOCKING AV MONOGRAM — DETAILED GEOMETRY

### Overall Shape Description
The monogram creates a **geometric illusion** of two flat ribbon-like bands woven through each other, forming an AV ligature inside a **diamond/rhombus silhouette**.

### Letter Identification
- **"A"**: Formed by the band that descends from top-center toward the LOWER-LEFT, then turns back right → creates the two legs of the letter A that spread apart at the bottom
- **"V"**: Formed by the band that descends from top-center toward the LOWER-RIGHT, forming the V shape → the two arms converge at the bottom

### 3D Ribbon Weave Effect
The two bands appear as flat ribbons with uniform width (~8-12% of card width) that pass OVER and UNDER each other at two crossing points:

1. **Upper crossing** (y_card ≈ 42-47%): The V-band's right arm passes BEHIND the A-band's left arm
   - Evidence: At y_card=42-46%, 3-4 fragmented groups appear as the right arm is occluded
   - At y_card=47-48%, the groups merge as bands run parallel

2. **Lower crossing** (y_card ≈ 54-58%): The A-band passes BEHIND the V-band
   - Evidence: At y_card=54-56%, the LEFT arm splits into a narrow leading edge + gap
   - The right arm remains continuous, indicating it's now in FRONT

### Construction Method (for Canvas2D)
The monogram is best constructed as **4 polygon sections** (2 "over" segments and 2 "under" segments):

```
Section 1: V-band top (FRONT) — apex down to first crossing
Section 2: A-band left (FRONT) — through first crossing zone  
Section 3: V-band bottom-right (FRONT) — through second crossing zone
Section 4: A-band bottom-left (BEHIND) — through second crossing zone

Drawing order:
  1. Draw Section 4 (A behind) with hatching
  2. Draw Section 2 (A front) with hatching → covers crossing 1
  3. Draw Section 1 (V top) with hatching
  4. Draw Section 3 (V front at crossing 2) with hatching → covers crossing 2
```

### Monogram Color
- **Line color**: `#ffffff` (pure white) for all hatching strokes — confirmed at multiple points
- **No solid fill** — the entire monogram is rendered purely through parallel hatching lines
- **The perceived "color" of the monogram** is the average of white lines + dark gaps = approximately `#c8c8c8` to `#e0e0e0` depending on viewing distance
- **Both letters use identical color** — no color differentiation between A and V; the interlocking is communicated purely through geometry (over/under occlusion)

### Transparency Effects
- **None on front** — pure white hatching on opaque dark background
- **On back (watermark)**: Lines are `#b8b8ba` on `#f8f9fb` → effective opacity ~25-30%
- The watermark uses the SAME hatching pattern but with gray lines instead of white, creating a subtle ghost/watermark effect

- **T1 (Name)**: ALL CAPS
- **T2 (Title)**: ALL CAPS
- **T3 (Address)**: ALL CAPS
- **T4 (Contact)**: Mixed — address/phone in CAPS, email/website in lowercase

### Key Typography Decisions
1. **Three weights, not sizes** — T2/T3/T4 all share the same size (2.0%); differentiation comes from weight and color
2. **Title uses extreme letter-spacing** (0.25em+) — this is the strongest typographic signal of luxury
3. **Name has moderate letter-spacing** (0.12em) — wide enough to feel premium, tight enough to read easily
4. **No serifs anywhere** — pure geometric sans creates a modern, architectural feeling

## 9. CANVAS2D RENDERING ORDER

### Front Side
1. **Fill background gradient**: `createLinearGradient(0, 0, cardW, 0)` with stops at 0% `#252628`, 50% `#3b3c3e`, 65% `#4b4c4e`, 100% `#3e3f41`
2. **Construct monogram paths**: Two interlocking ribbon-band polygons (Band A and Band B)
3. **For each band section** (in correct z-order for over/under):
   a. `ctx.save(); ctx.beginPath();` trace band section polygon
   b. `ctx.clip();`
   c. Draw 45° parallel lines: `strokeStyle = '#ffffff'`, `lineWidth = 0.5% * cardW`, spacing = `1.5% * cardW`
   d. `ctx.restore();`

### Back Side
1. **Fill background**: `ctx.fillStyle = '#f8f9fb'; ctx.fillRect(0, 0, cardW, cardH);`
2. **Draw watermark monogram**: Same geometry as front, scaled ~2.2x, positioned at center (64%, 48%)
   - Clip to scaled monogram paths
   - Draw 45° hatching with `strokeStyle = '#b8b8ba'`, same proportional spacing
3. **Draw name**: `"ROB SIMAX"`, font `700 ${0.033*cardH}px Futura`, color `#1c1d1e`, at (8.8%W, 12.6%H), letterSpacing `0.12em`
4. **Draw title**: `"ARTIST"`, font `300 ${0.020*cardH}px Futura`, color `#838587`, at (9.0%W, 21.0%H), letterSpacing `0.25em`
5. **Draw address**: `"BOULEVARD 01234"`, font `300 ${0.020*cardH}px Futura`, color `#8a8b8d`, at (9.0%W, 77.2%H)
6. **Draw contact1**: Left `"LONDON, MAIN STREET, BIG BUILDING A/21"` + Right `"+ 01 (0) 234 567 890"`, font `400 ${0.020*cardH}px Futura`, color `#939495`, at y=82.5%H
7. **Draw contact2**: Left `"contact@info.com"` + Right `"www.rob_simax.com"`, same font, color `#7c7d7f`, at y=87.8%H

## 10. DESIGN DNA SUMMARY

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Monochrome mastery** | Zero accent colors — entire palette is achromatic gray scale |
| **Engraving technique** | 45° hatching simulates traditional copperplate engraving — luxury craft signal |
| **Impossible geometry** | Penrose-triangle-like interlocking creates visual intrigue |
| **Extreme restraint** | Front = monogram only. Back = text only. Zero decoration. |
| **Yin-yang duality** | Dark front ↔ light back — maximum drama from minimum palette |
| **Weight hierarchy** | Bold → Regular → Light creates reading order without size change |
| **Negative space** | 54% of back card is intentionally empty — confidence, not scarcity |
| **Watermark echo** | Front's monogram reappears as ghost on back — brand cohesion |
