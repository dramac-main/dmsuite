## DESIGN DNA â€” What Makes This Card Unique

### 1. Diagonal Corner Bracket Pair
The most distinctive element. Only **two** L-brackets appear â€” top-left and bottom-right â€” creating an **implied diagonal axis** across the card. This is NOT a four-corner frame. The asymmetry generates visual tension and movement without adding complexity. It frames the content zone through suggestion rather than enclosure.

### 2. Semantic Color Dots
Instead of icons (phone/email/pin), tiny colored **dots** differentiate contact channels. Each color has a psychological association:
- ðŸŸ  Orange (`#FF6B35`) â†’ Phone (warm, personal, call-to-action)
- ðŸŸ¢ Green (`#4CAF50`) â†’ Email (digital, go/available)
- ðŸ”µ Blue (`#2196F3`) â†’ Address (location, trust, corporate)

This is more abstract than icons â€” the viewer maps meaning through position context, not symbol recognition. Ultra-minimal.

### 3. Extreme Typographic Hierarchy
The system uses **five gray values** for text (`#2D2D2D` â†’ `#888888` â†’ `#999999` â†’ `#AAAAAA` â†’ `#CCCCCC`) creating a clear visual hierarchy without bold/italic/underline variation. The name is the only element with weight 600; everything else uses weight 300â€“400. Letter-spacing does the heavy lifting for the name (0.18em) vs. contact (0.01em).

### 4. Front/Back Duality
The front (white) and back (dark `#1A1A1A`) create a **yin-yang duality**. The back's centered frame echoes the front's diagonal brackets â€” both use thin gray lines as structural elements, but with opposite figure/ground relationships.

### 5. Generous Whitespace Ratio
Content occupies roughly **35%** of the front surface. The remaining 65% is pure white. This breathing room is deliberate â€” it makes the tiny dots and thin brackets feel precious and intentional rather than empty.

### 6. No Icons, No Gradients, No Shadows
Zero ornamentation beyond the brackets, dots, and QR code. No social media icons, no decorative dividers, no drop shadows, no rounded corners. Every element earns its place.
