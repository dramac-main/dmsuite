## DESIGN DNA

### How the Angular Composition Works

1. **Front side — Asymmetric Division**: The card is split diagonally — clean white (creative space / name) in the upper-left, dark charcoal (authority / contact info) in the lower-right. The diagonal creates dynamic energy and makes the card feel modern rather than static. The steep angle (~60 degrees) gives a forward-leaning, progressive feel.

2. **Red/Orange-to-Magenta Gradient Bar**: This small but vivid element acts as a "flag" or brand marker. It's positioned at the top-left where the eye starts, providing an immediate color hook. The gradient adds depth and prevents it from looking flat. The warm-to-cool shift (orange to magenta) creates chromatic tension.

3. **Back side — Converging Angular Shapes**: Two dominant shapes (charcoal + lime) overlap and converge toward a central point, creating:
   - **Visual tension**: The shapes point downward like arrows, directing the eye
   - **Depth through overlap**: The olive transition zone between dark and lime creates a 3D layered effect
   - **The zigzag edge**: The dark shape's right boundary doesn't follow a smooth diagonal — it zigzags with at least 3 major direction changes (at y=17%, 29%, 35%), creating visual complexity and preventing monotony

4. **Color Contrast Strategy**:
   - **Charcoal (#303030)** provides weight, seriousness, authority
   - **Lime (#D0E85C)** provides energy, freshness, modernity — it's a yellow-green (chartreuse) not a blue-green
   - **White (#FFFFFF)** provides breathing room and cleanliness
   - **The gradient bar (orange to magenta)** adds a warm counterpoint to the cool lime
   - **Olive (#7E8D37)** overlap creates natural depth

5. **Compositional Balance**:
   - Front: ~60% white / 40% dark — elegant restraint
   - Back: ~40% shapes / ~60% white — shapes concentrated at top, text space below
   - The shapes angle downward, leading the eye toward the content area

### What Makes It Effective
- **High contrast**: Pure white against charcoal creates maximum readability
- **Limited palette**: Only 3 core hues (charcoal, lime, red-gradient) prevent visual chaos
- **Angular geometry**: Creates movement without animation — the eye follows the diagonals
- **Asymmetry**: Avoids static/boring centered layouts
- **The zigzag edge**: Adds organic irregularity to what would otherwise be sterile geometric shapes — makes it memorable
- **Convergence points**: Both the front diagonal and back shapes converge to focal points, creating visual anchors
